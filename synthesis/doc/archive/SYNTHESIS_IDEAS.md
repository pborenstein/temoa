## Wild Synthesis Project Ideas

### 1. Personal Knowledge Graph Visualizer
**Concept**: Interactive network maps showing how interests, references, and daily thoughts connect over time.

**What it would show**:
- "Leonard Cohen" → "mortality" → "Judaism" → specific films/books → personal reflections as living web
- Temporal evolution of concept clusters
- Unexpected connection discovery
- Interest intersection points

**Technical approach**: 
- Extract entity relationships from daily notes and references
- Use semantic embeddings for concept similarity
- Create interactive D3.js or similar visualization
- Time-based animation of knowledge graph evolution

### 2. The Mood-Content Oracle
**Concept**: Correlate daily note sentiment/content with references saved, media consumed, health patterns.

**What it predicts**:
- Creative periods based on historical patterns
- Optimal content suggestions for current mood
- Health-creativity correlations
- "You're in a Tarkovsky mood" type recommendations

**Technical approach**:
- Sentiment analysis on daily notes
- Pattern matching between mood indicators and content consumption
- Machine learning on historical correlations
- Real-time mood detection from recent notes

### 3. Temporal Interest Archaeology
**Concept**: Mine vault for how interests evolve over time.

**What it reveals**:
- Interest lifelines from birth to peak to evolution
- Concept genealogy ("AI/LLMs" journey through vault)
- Dormant interests that might resurface
- Influence patterns between different topics

**Example analysis**: Track "AI/LLMs" from first mention → daily notes → gleanings → Reference/Tech files → creative writing, showing complete interest evolution.

### 4. The Great Synthesis Engine
**Concept**: LLM-powered system finding unexpected connections across vault.

**What it discovers**:
- 1940s film review ↔ daily note about father ↔ tech reference about documentation
- Hidden thematic threads spanning years
- Unconscious recurring patterns
- Cross-domain concept bridges

**Technical approach**:
- Semantic similarity search across all content
- LLM-powered connection explanation generation
- Theme extraction and cross-referencing
- Serendipitous discovery algorithms

### 5. Personal Culture Recommender
**Concept**: Recommender that actually knows your taste, not just collaborative filtering.

**What it knows**:
- Your thoughts about mortality in Bergman films
- Tuesday evening mood patterns
- How you react to specific directors/authors/topics
- Context-dependent preferences

**Recommendations like**:
- "Based on your Thursday melancholy and recent Electrelane obsession, try..."
- "You liked X for reason Y, here are other works with similar Y qualities"
- "Your 'father issues' tag suggests you'd appreciate..."

### 6. The Conversation Generator
**Concept**: Create synthetic conversations between different versions of yourself.

**Possible conversations**:
- "2024 Philip" debating "2025 Philip" on retirement, creativity, mortality
- Past-you responding to present-day concerns
- Imaginary dialogues between your film thoughts and personal reflections
- "What would I have said about X before experiencing Y?"

### 7. Serendipity Machine
**Concept**: Smart random-walk generator through vault creating discovery paths.

**How it works**:
- Start with current mood/interest
- Walk through related content with weighted randomness
- Surface forgotten connections
- Create "adventure" paths through your own mind

**Sample path**: Today's agitation → 2024 note about Gil → Leonard Cohen reference → mortality themes → specific film → forgotten insight about fathers

### 8. Personal Mythology Mapper
**Concept**: Extract recurring themes, symbols, people, places to build your personal mythology.

**What it identifies**:
- Your personal archetypes and recurring stories
- Symbolic language patterns
- Mythological structures in daily life
- Personal legend development over time

**Output**: Visual mythology map showing your recurring symbols, relationships, themes, growth patterns.

### 9. The Context Oracle
**Concept**: Real-time relevant context surfacing while consuming media or writing.

**While watching a film**: Surfaces your previous thoughts on director, similar themes, related daily notes
**While writing**: Suggests relevant past insights, supporting references, thematic connections
**While reading**: Shows how this connects to your existing knowledge web

### 10. Legacy Synthesis Reports
**Concept**: Periodic "What Have I Been Thinking About?" reports synthesizing periods of life.

**Report types**:
- Monthly thematic evolution summaries
- Quarterly creativity pattern analysis
- Yearly interest archaeology reports
- Decade perspective synthesis

**Sample report**: "August 2025: Increased focus on mortality themes (5 mentions), declining tech optimism (down 40%), peak creative output on Tuesdays, strong correlation between Gil-anxiety and film consumption"
