## 2025-04-02 

- Kind of an odd day, where it's almost 4 and I'm not sure what I've done besides eating lunch.
- I watched [[Below the Fray - The Charlie Rose Paradox]]
- I don't usually watch this sort of thing. For some reason I think that watching YouTubes is boring. On the other hand, it does seem to be the way people are making art.
- I don't know who this Beyond the Fray guy is. What I find fascinating is the production. I work on the same thing for months and make little progress. Guys like that seem to crank out good content as a side job. I know: comparison is the thief of joy. But still, [[scale|the world cannot support so many ballerinas]] 
- I find that I keep worrying about the Pulitzer before I've written anything. This is something that I can probably find in my Journals from college.
- So, what was my life about after all that?

- It's a little surprising to see that my work habits are the same no matter who I am working for. I thought I procrastinated (did a lot of other stuff) because I was avoiding doing the work which I assumed I didn't like.
- I figured that if I was doing work I liked 
	- ![[not a good employee]]
- I figured that if I was doing work I liked, I would just get to it. There would be no "but first I need to do this," and "before that," and etc. I thought I was doing all that preflightering in order to avoid work that I didn't like.
- But No. I was avoiding work.
- Didn't matter whether I liked it or not. It was always going to take me until 3pm to get started. If I was going to get started during daylight at all.
- I imagined that once I wasn't doing work I didn't like, I would become the kind of worker I was told I should have been. I imagined schedules. And plans. And my calendar would have 10-11:30 Obsidian Project / 11:30-12:30 Lunch / 12:30-2:30 Read all over it. And instead of it feeling oppressive, like it did when I worked for others, now it would feel liberating to have a plan for myself. It somehow didn't occur to me that all the planning and stuff and having to go to meetings and stuff was the stuff that I didn't like in the first place.
- I always (?) said: The domain doesn't matter. I want to work with interesting people doing interesting things.
- There was a time when I was drawing 24-hr clocks and shading in where I slept, where I worked. ANy time I did this exercise, I got anxious. There just wasn't enough time in the day to do anything. Nonetheless, I managed to waste it.
